Join the American Legion Family

The American Legion is the nation's largest wartime veterans service organization, committed to mentoring youth, sponsoring wholesome programs in our communities, advocating patriotism and honor, promoting strong national security, and continued devotion to our fellow service members and veterans.

Eligibility

Membership in The American Legion is open to all U.S. military veterans who served at least one day of active military duty during the following eligible war eras, and were honorably discharged or are still serving honorably:

  • World War I (April 6, 1917 – November 11, 1918)
  • World War II (December 7, 1941 – December 31, 1946)
  • Korean War (June 25, 1950 – January 31, 1955)
  • Vietnam War (February 28, 1961 – May 7, 1975)
  • Lebanon/Grenada (August 24, 1982 – July 31, 1984)
  • Panama (December 20, 1989 – January 31, 1990)
  • Gulf War/War on Terrorism (August 2, 1990 – present)

Member Benefits

Camaraderie

Connect with fellow veterans who understand your experiences

Advocacy

Your voice amplified on Capitol Hill for veterans' issues

Discounts

Exclusive discounts on products, services, and insurance

Assistance

Help with VA claims and accessing benefits you've earned

Annual Dues

Annual membership dues help support our programs and services. Contact our post for current dues information.

How to Join

  1. Verify your eligibility based on the criteria above
  2. Complete a Membership Application
  3. Submit your application with a copy of your DD-214 or other proof of service
  4. Pay your annual dues
  5. Attend a meeting and get involved!
